Residential Epoxy FAQ

How much does residential epoxy flooring cost in Connecticut?

Residential epoxy flooring typically costs $3-8 per square foot depending on the coating system, surface preparation needs, and design complexity. Garage floors average $1,500-3,500 for a standard 2-car garage. We provide free detailed estimates for all Connecticut homeowners.

How long does epoxy flooring last in a home?

Professional epoxy floors last 20+ years with proper maintenance. This is 400% longer than garage floor paint which typically lasts 3-5 years. Our installations are backed by our lifetime support guarantee.

Is epoxy flooring safe for indoor use?

Yes! Once cured, epoxy floors are completely safe, non-toxic, and emit zero VOCs. They're perfect for basements, kitchens, laundry rooms, and living spaces. We use low-VOC formulations during installation.

Can epoxy be applied over existing floors?

Epoxy can be applied over concrete, but we must first remove any existing coatings, sealers, or paint. We diamond-grind the surface to ensure proper adhesion. Old tile or vinyl must be removed first.

How long before I can use my floor after installation?

You can walk on epoxy floors after 24 hours. For vehicle traffic (garages), we recommend waiting 72 hours. Full cure takes 7 days, after which your floor reaches maximum durability and chemical resistance.

Does epoxy flooring work in Connecticut winters?

Absolutely! Epoxy is highly temperature tolerant and performs excellently in Connecticut's freeze-thaw cycles. It won't crack from temperature changes and makes snow removal easier with its smooth, durable surface.
