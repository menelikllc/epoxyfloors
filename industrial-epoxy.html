Industrial Epoxy FAQ

How thick should industrial epoxy be?

Industrial epoxy thickness varies by application. Standard systems are 10-20 mils, heavy-duty systems are 60-125 mils, and extreme applications use 1/8" to 1/4" thick systems. We specify thickness based on your equipment loads and chemical exposure.

Can epoxy withstand forklift traffic?

Yes! Industrial epoxy systems are specifically engineered for forklift traffic. Thicker systems with aggregate reinforcement handle constant forklift use, pallet impacts, and turning forces without delaminating or cracking.

What chemicals can epoxy resist?

Industrial epoxy resists most acids, alkalis, solvents, oils, and industrial chemicals. Specific resistance depends on the formulation. We test chemical compatibility and recommend the appropriate system for your specific chemicals.

Do you work around production schedules?

Absolutely! We coordinate with your operations to minimize downtime. We can work nights, weekends, during shutdowns, or in phases. Fast-cure systems allow return to service in as little as 12-24 hours.
